Neptune

-At the top of its clouds, temperatures on Neptune can dip down to -221.45 degrees Celsius.

-Has the strongest winds in the Solar System. The speed record reached 2,000km/h

-If you could stand on the surface, the force of gravity is almost the same as on Earth.

-Year: 165 Earth years.

-Day: 16 h 6 min

-Mass: 1.02413×1026kg; 17.147 Earths

-Diameter: 24,341±30km; 3.829 Earths

-Planet Type: Ice Giant

-Moons: 14

Mars

-Average temperature is -63 degrees Celsius.

The gravity is about 38% of Earth’s gravity.

-Through continuous observations and analysis, it is strongly believed that Mars was once very similar to Earth, possessing water or even whole oceans.

-Mars is home to the tallest mountain in the solar system Olympus Mons, a shield volcano, is 21km high and 600km in diameter.

-Year: 687 Earth Days

-Day: 24h 37m

-Mass‎: ‎6.4171×1023kg; 0.107 Earths

-Diameter: 6 791km

-Type: Terrestrial

-Moons: 2

Saturn

-Saturn’s largest moon, Titan, is the only moon in the Solar System to possess an atmosphere.

-The lightest planet in our solar system and has very low density so if you could put it on a water it would float.

-Saturn spins so quickly on its axis that the planet flattens itself out into an oblate spheroid. Seriously, you see this by eye when you look at a picture of Saturn; it looks like someone squished the planet a little. Of course, it’s the rapid spinning that’s squishing it, causing the equator to bulge out.

-Year: 30 Earth years

-Day: 10h 39min

-Mass: 5.6834×1026kg; 95.159 Earths

-Diameter: 120 536km

-Type: Gas giant

-Moons: 53 confirmed | 29 provisional

Mercury

-Mercury is so close to the sun that daytime temperatures reach 400°C and because it has almost no atmosphere to trap heat its -170 °C at night.

-Although it is the closest planet to the sun, it is not the hottest one.

-Has the shortest year.

-Mercury has just 38% the gravity of Earth, this is too little to hold on to what atmosphere it has which is blown away by solar winds.

-Year: 88 Earth days

-Day: 176 Earth days

-Mass‎: 3,285e23 kg; 0,055 Earths

-Diameter: 2,440km

-Type: Terrestrial

-Moons: 0

The Sun

-Over one million Earth’s could fit inside the Sun.

-The Sun is 4.5 billion years old that means it's half way through its life. Currently known as a Yellow Dwarf.

-Light from the Sun reaches Earth in around 8 minutes.

-Sun’s outer surface glows bright enough to burn your retinas, the Sun’s core is pitch black.

-One day the Sun will consume the Earth.

-Mass‎: 1,989e30 kg; 330,000 earths

-Diameter: 695,508 km

-Type: Yellow dwarf

Venus

-The temperature at the surface is higher than 470 degrees Celsius.

-The Soviet Venera probes that ventured to the surface decades ago didn’t last more than two hours.

-While Venus turns really slowly on its axis every 243 days and It's slowing down. While its upper atmosphere whips around the planet underneath it every 4 days.

-Venus has a bizarre rotation because its spins backwards compared to the other planets.

-Year: 225 Earth days

-Day: 243 Earth days

-Mass‎: 4,867e24 kg; 0,815 Earths

-Diameter: 6,052 km

-Type: Terrestrial

-Moons: 0

Jupiter

-It has a very fast spin rate among all the planets in our solar system. It spins at a speed of 50,000 kilometers per hour.

-The temperature near the planet's core may be about 23871 degrees celsius thats hotter than the surface of the sun.

-Has the most moons of any planet in the solar system.

-Has the shortest day of all the planets.

-Year: 11.86 Earth Years

-Day: 9h 55min

-Mass‎: 1,898e27 kg; 318 Earths

-Diameter: 142,984 km

-Type: Gas Giant

-Moons: 53 confirmed | 26 provisional

Earth

-70% of the Earth’s Surface is Covered in Water.

-The Earth’s Atmosphere Extends to a Distance of 10,000km.

-The Earth’s rotation is gradually slowing at approximately 17 milliseconds per hundred years. This is effecting lenght of our days, but it happens so slowly that it could take around 140 million years before the length of a day will have increased to 25 hours.

-Year: 365.25 days

-Day: 23.9 hours

-Mass‎: 5,972e24kg

-Diameter: 6,371 km

-Moons: 1

Uranus

-The average surface temperature of Uranus is -224°C making it the coldest planet in the solar system.

-Because of the unique tilt of the planet, a night at one of its poles lasts for 21 Earth years, during which it will receive no heat or light from the Sun at all.

-Uranus is the 7th planet away from the Sun, and by diameter, is the third largest.

-Year: 84 Earth years

-Day: 17 h 14 min

-Mass‎: 8,681e25 kg; 14,54 Earths

-Diameter: 25,362 km

-Planet Type: Ice Giant

-Moons: 27
